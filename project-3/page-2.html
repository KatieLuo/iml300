What is the Asian American experience?

Try search up "Asian American" and your search browser becomes flooded with dozens of articles from 2021. Most of them sprouting from the 2021 Atlanta spa shooting where six Asian women were killed. Notice that before then, there's little trace of what it means to be Asian American on the internet. Likewise, this lack of representation can be seen across all American media. Despite a growing Asian American population of 19 million residents, Western media still struggles to depict the Asian American experience.

Perhaps this lack of representation is not just due to the agonizingly slow process of representation in the entertainment industry, but also the confusion about what the Asian American experiece is actually about. And that in itself is no easy question to answer. If we disect the term Asian American, we have "Asian" and "American", connotating a mixed experience of both idenities. Asia covers covers roughly 4.6 billion people and 48 countries; however, colloquially, "Asian" means East Asian. Pop culture and movies assume films such as "Crazy Rich Asians" adequate representation of Asians, but this is obviously not the case. The term Asian American houses a plethora of idneities and experiences. And is also the most underrepresented and misunderstood.
